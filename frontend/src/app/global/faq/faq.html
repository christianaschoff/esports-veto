Frequently Asked Questions

Is the service free?

Yes. For the time being the service is free without limitation.
There are no plans to change that right now.

Are there limits how many vetos I can create?

No. There are no limits right now.
But there are rate limits, so don't hammer against the endpoints ;)

How log are my vetos stored?

The veto information is stored for 45 days, starting after the creation of the veto process.
Why 45 days? Its a gut feeling. Lets find out together, if thats a good timespan or not.

Why is there no account where all my vetos are stored?

Since we want to store no personal data, we did not implement any kind of account registration.
In addition our goal is to see what are the limits to providing a service without pinpointing it to individual users.
That being said - Maybe we will integrate third party accounts to enhance the user experience for you.

What data do you store?

As litte as possible. Of course we need to store data to run the service.
We store all the data regarding the veto configuration (including Title and Player/Team names - what is totally optional!).
We use a Open Telemetry compliant interface to monitor the health of the system an log transit data.
We proivide the option for observers to store animation configuration locally in LocalStorage within your browser, so you don't need to configure the animations for each veto process all over again - if you want to.
We use a JWT (Jvascript Web Token) to do rate limiting and anoymously authenticate you as a user. This lays the groundwork to connect the veto system to other services in the future.

I have lost my link/QRCode... what now?

We do not store any account data.
If you are a player or streamer streamer, you can contact the admin of the veto process.
They might send you the data again.

If you are the admin of the veto process:
You must create a new veto process and share the links/QR Code with participants.
That is the downside storing as less data as possible.

Can I use the veto system in my (commercial) streams / events?

Yes. You can use the service and integrate it into your streams.
You can grab the whole page (but be carefull with your browsers url!) or parts of it.
All attributions are appriciated of course ;).
